1950
The Turing Test
Alan Turing asks: "Can machines think?"
The Imitation Game proposes that a machine is intelligent if it can fool a human into thinking it is also human.
1956
The Dartmouth Workshop
The term "Artificial Intelligence" is officially born.
The founding event of AI as a field, bringing together McCarthy, Minsky, Rochester, and Shannon.
1958
The Perceptron
Frank Rosenblatt creates the first neural network model.
An algorithm for supervised learning of binary classifiers, the foundation of neural networks.
1960s-70s

The Awakening

The first golden age of AI. ELIZA simulates conversation, Shakey the Robot navigates the physical world, and symbolic AI dominates. But optimism meets reality as computational limits trigger the first AI Winter.

1966
ELIZA
The first chatbot simulates a psychotherapist.
Weizenbaum's creation showed that simple pattern matching could create the illusion of understanding.
1969
Shakey the Robot
First general-purpose mobile robot with reasoning.
Shakey combined locomotion, perception, and problem-solving in one platform.
1974
First AI Winter
Reality checks and funding cuts freeze progress.
The Lighthill Report and funding cuts lead to a decade of reduced AI research.
1980s

The Expert Systems

Knowledge becomes power. Rule-based expert systems like MYCIN and XCON bring AI into the corporate world. Japan's Fifth Generation Computer Project ignites global competition, but the bubble bursts again by decade's end.

1980
XCON & MYCIN
Expert systems prove AI's commercial value.
Rule-based systems dominate commercial AI applications, from medicine to computer configuration.
1982
Fifth Generation Project
Japan's ambitious AI computer initiative.
A national project that reshaped global computing strategy despite not meeting all objectives.
1987
Second AI Winter
The collapse of the Lisp machine market.
Specialized hardware becomes obsolete as general-purpose computers catch up.
1990s

The Quiet Rise

Machine learning emerges from the shadows. Statistical methods replace hard-coded rules. IBM's Deep Blue defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov, proving machines can master human intellect at its highest level.

1997
Deep Blue vs Kasparov
AI defeats the world chess champion.
A landmark moment where specialized hardware and search algorithms beat human strategic thinking.
1998
LeNet-5
Convolutional networks prove their worth.
LeCun's architecture for handwritten digit recognition lays groundwork for modern computer vision.
1990s
Statistical Revolution
Machine learning overtakes symbolic AI.
Data-driven approaches begin to dominate over hand-coded knowledge bases.
2000s

The Learning Era

The internet unleashes a tsunami of data. Geoffrey Hinton's deep learning revival, the ImageNet challenge, and kernel methods transform pattern recognition. AI begins to permeate everyday technology silently but surely.

2006
Deep Learning Revival
Hinton reignites neural network research.
Layer-wise pre-training makes deep neural networks feasible again.
2009
ImageNet Dataset
Big data meets computer vision.
A massive labeled dataset that becomes the benchmark for visual recognition.
2000s
The Rise of Big Data
AI becomes an invisible utility.
Search engines, recommendations, and translation quietly transform daily life.
2010s

The Deep Revolution

AlexNet's ImageNet victory ignites the deep learning explosion. GPUs become AI accelerators. AlphaGo defeats the world's best Go player. Transformers emerge, setting the stage for the generative AI era. AI goes from academic curiosity to global strategic priority.

2012
AlexNet Breakthrough
Deep learning conquers computer vision.
The ImageNet 2012 victory that convinced the world deep learning was the future.
2016
AlphaGo vs Lee Sedol
AI masters the ancient game of Go.
A historic match watched by 280 million people, proving AI can master intuition.
2017
The Transformer
"Attention Is All You Need" changes everything.
The architecture that makes modern large language models possible.
2014
GANs
AI learns to create, not just classify.
Adversarial training opens the door to generative AI applications.
2020s

The Intelligence Explosion

GPT-3 demonstrates emergent capabilities. ChatGPT captures the world's attention, becoming the fastest-growing application in history. Multimodal AI generates images, video, and code from text. The race toward AGI begins, bringing both wonder and existential questions.

2020
GPT-3
Scale unlocks emergent intelligence.
175 billion parameters demonstrate that bigger models can do things no one trained them to do.
2022
ChatGPT
AI becomes a household name.
The fastest-growing consumer application in history changes how the world views AI.
2023
The Multimodal Era
AI sees, hears, and creates.
Models break the text barrier and begin processing the full spectrum of human media.
2024+
The Path to AGI
The final frontier approaches.
The race toward general intelligence raises humanity's biggest questions.
